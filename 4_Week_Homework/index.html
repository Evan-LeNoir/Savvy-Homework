    Performance

    • Engine: Turbocharged 2.5L DFI 5-cylinder, sourced by Audi.
    • Power: 330whp/380lb-tq. @1600RPM. Limited to 8700RPM
    • Layout:
      • Front-mid Engine, Rear Wheel Drive.
    • Electronic Config. Options:
      • ABS, ESP, LC, Drive-by-Wire and Digital Motronics (Note; Race models only had necessary computers for the engine and telemetry.)

    Chassis

    • Frame:
      • Hand-crafted tubular space-frame.
    • Suspension:
      • Double-Wishbone front; Multi-link rear. Inboard bell-crank shock-towers.
    • Steering:
      • Electronic redux gear (Road models). Rack and pinion (RS models).
    • Curb Weight:
      • 1,356lb

    Pedigree

    • Officially branded in 1978 by Joop Donkervoort
    • Made Nördchleife's top 10 fastest cars to lap all 12mi with a time of 00:07:14.9 in the Production Cars category.
    • The D8 GT (the predecessor to the GTO) is still known as the world's lightest GT Comp' Car due to its extensive use of carbon fibre and next-level architectural engineering with its space-frame.
    • The D8 GT also made Donkervoort a well known and feared staple in the GT class after decimating the competition at 24hrs of Dubai in 2011.

    Donkervoort D8 GTO (2013–present)

    The GTO was a complete and risky departure from Donkervoort's usual tried and true crafting procedures. In years prior, this model would show up to unveilings at prestigious autoshows such as Geneva, Frankfurt and Michigan but despite how many times they were asked, nothing ever came of it. That all changed in winter of 2013 when Donkervoort announced there would be a new model to replace the aging D8 GT which had become the brand's halo-car and vicious contender in the GT Car completions as well as LéMan. This new model had some big shoes to fill.

    Starting with the GTO Premium, as a sort of way to test market interest and gain valuable feedback from loyal customers as well as their racing teams, just 25 examples being made. These Premium models received a power bump that surpasses even the current GTO Performance model and loaded to the gills with every option offered at the time. The result was an unexpected monumental success straight out of left field!
